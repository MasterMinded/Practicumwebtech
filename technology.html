The Turing Test is a test conducted on machines to see if the machines can behave and respond intelligently like a human. The test is held as an important obstacle for machines to pass, and once the machine passes the machine is on the route towards true intelligence. Artificial intelligence is aimed towards being human with the same thought process and responses. This is to show that even behind a computer screen and keyboard, the judge (you) are able to have a conversation and articulate and ask questions to the A.I. just like you would to any human being. Once these questions are asked, the A.I. would respond just like how any human would in a regular conversation, thus passing the Turing Test, but if the responses are obscured compare to a typical answer than it would not pass. The key problem is that computers don’t have the intelligence of humans, but have specialized intelligence for specific tasks. It also raises ethical issues such as building a robot that is more intelligent than humans or be given jobs that humans can do. Thus, to allow computers with A.I. they must pass the Turing Test without the human on the other side of the keyboard to know that they are in fact a robot.
